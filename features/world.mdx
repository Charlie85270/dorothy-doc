Agent World (also known as "Pallet Town" or "ClaudeMon") is a playable 2D game world built right into Dorothy. Inspired by classic Pokemon games, it turns your agents into NPC characters that you can walk up to and interact with.

<Note>
Agent World is purely for fun and visualization. It doesn't change how your agents work or affect their performance in any way. Think of it as a creative way to see what your agents are up to.
</Note>

## How It Works

When you open Agent World, you'll find yourself in a tile-based 2D world. Your agents appear as NPC characters scattered around the map. You can:

- **Walk around** the world using your keyboard
- **Talk to agent NPCs** to see what they're currently working on, their status, and recent activity
- **Explore buildings** and enter interiors with their own maps and NPCs
- **Read signs** placed around the world with helpful information

## Zones

The game world is divided into **zones** -- distinct areas with their own maps, characters, and themes. Each zone has:

- A tile-based map with terrain, buildings, and decorations
- NPCs representing your agents or other characters
- Interactive signs with text you can read
- Collision detection so you can't walk through walls or objects

### Generative Zones

One of the most unique features is the ability to generate entirely new zones using AI. Dorothy can create custom zones with:

- A theme you specify (e.g., "a cozy library", "a futuristic lab", "a forest village")
- Auto-generated maps, NPCs, and dialogue
- Custom signs with relevant information

This means your game world can grow and evolve as you add more agents and projects.

<Tip>
Create themed zones for different projects or teams. For example, a "Backend Zone" for your backend agents and a "Frontend Zone" for your frontend agents.
</Tip>

## Technical Details

Under the hood, Agent World uses:

- Tile-based rendering with sprite sheets
- Collision detection for walls, buildings, and objects
- A dialogue system for NPC conversations
- Building interiors as separate maps you can enter and exit

## Managing the World

You can manage zones, NPCs, and signs through Dorothy's MCP tools. For the full list of available commands, see the [World MCP Reference](/reference/mcp-world).
